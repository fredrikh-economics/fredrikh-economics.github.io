About

I am a Senior Research Fellow at the Research Institute of Industrial Economics (IFN) and Associate Professor of Economics. My research spans international economics, globalization, foreign direct investment and firm dynamics, with a particular focus on how multinational activity and technological change affect wages, inequality, mobility and innovation. A recurring theme in my work is how global forces—trade, offshoring, foreign ownership and automation—reshape labor market outcomes and the allocation of talent across firms and occupations. I regularly collaborate with researchers across countries and disciplines and contribute to policy discussions on globalization, productivity and labor markets.
